Lost at Sea, 1803

Return of the Obra Dinn: a study of music

insured by the East India Company

ATTENTION:This game is best played without any prior knowledge.
I will do my best to avoid major spoilers, but even if you're just vaguely curious about playing it, please go do so before continuing.

In 1802, the merchant ship Obra Dinn set out from London for the Orient with over 200 tons of trade goods. Six months later it hadn't met its rendezvous point at the Cape of Good Hope and was declared lost at sea.

Early this morning of October 14th, 1807, the Obra Dinn drifted into port at Falmouth with damaged sails and no visible crew. As insurance investigator for the East India Company's London Office, dispatch immediately to Falmouth, find means to board the ship, and prepare an assessment of damages.

The player is the insurance investigator, tasked by not only the East India Company but also a mysterious person and former member of the ship named Henry Evans who grants the player a logbook journal as well as a Memento Mortem pocketwatch to view the last moments of the passengers and crew. There are 60 fates to be explored. The voyage is split into seven chapters, which tell a linear story while being presented in a non-linear fashion.

Each chapter, which have two sections that can be accessed, have different songs for each section. A comprehensive collection of all the songs can be found on Lucas Pope's Spotify which includes additional tracks not found in the game.
